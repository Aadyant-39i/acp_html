Mumbai

Mumbai! India's city of dreams. A world of possibilities that beckons those who dare to dream big. A bustling metropolis. A melting pot of diversity. A commercial hub. An entertainment capital, where a galaxy of stars and celebrities jostle for the spotlight.

Yet, Mumbai is so much more. A city with a past so rich that it is home to not one, not two but three UNESCO World Heritage Sites. And yet, it has a present that's equally fascinating, with lofty skyscrapers and glossy towers rising tall amidst beautiful colonial architecture and Victorian buildings.

Places to visit


  • The Gateway of India, which was built in 1924 to welcome King George V, this iconic basalt arch overlooking the Arabian Sea is a major historical landmark and meeting point.


  • Elephanta caves: A UNESCO World Heritage site reachable by boat, featuring ancient rock-cut Hindu and Buddhist temples on Elephanta Island.


  • Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSMT), a UNESCO site known for its stunning Victorian Gothic architecture, is a bustling, historic railway station.


  • Juhu Beach: A wide sandy shore famous for its lively carnival atmosphere, evening horse rides, and iconic stalls serving local spicy street food.


  • Sanjay Gandhi National Park: A vast green forest offering fresh air, cycling paths, and open-air safaris right in the middle of the busy city.


  • Global Vipassana Pagoda: A massive, silent golden pagoda dome that provides a serene space for meditation and offers panoramic views of the Gorai coast.

  • And many more!
