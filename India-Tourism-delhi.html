Delhi

Delhi's most famous monuments include UNESCO World Heritage sites like the Red Fort, Qutub Minar, and Humayun's Tomb, alongside iconic landmarks such as India Gate, Jama Masjid, and the Lotus Temple. They reflect a blend of Mughal, Persian, and colonial architecture, representing the city's rich, layered history

Iconic Historical Monuments


  • Red Fort (Lal Qila): Built by Shah Jahan in the 17th century, this red sandstone fort was the main residence of Mughal emperors.


  • Qutub Minar: A 73-meter high, five-story tower constructed in the early 13th century, it is the tallest brick minaret in India.


  • Humayun's Tomb: Built in the 16th century, this tomb is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a precursor to the Taj Mahal's architectural style.


  • Jama Masjid: One of India's largest mosques, it was built by Shah Jahan and can hold 25,000 worshippers.


  • India Gate: A 42-meter high war memorial dedicated to Indian and British soldiers who died in World War I.


  • Jantar Mantar: An astronomical observatory built by Maharaja Jai Singh II of Jaipur in 1724.


  • And many, many others!
