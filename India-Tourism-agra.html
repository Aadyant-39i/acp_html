Agra

Agra: Once the resplendent capital of the Mughal Empire under Akbar and Shah Jahan, this historic city on the banks of the Yamuna is home to three UNESCO World Heritage sites. It serves as a living museum of India's golden age, blending Persian-style gardens with monumental red sandstone and white marble architecture.


  • Taj Mahal: Commissioned in 1632 by Shah Jahan as a memorial for his wife Mumtaz Mahal, this ivory-white marble mausoleum took over 20 years and 20,000 artisans to complete.


  • Agra Fort: Originally a brick fortress known as Badalgarh, it was rebuilt in red sandstone by Akbar in 1565 and later served as the royal residence and prison for Shah Jahan.

  • Fatehpur Sikri: Founded by Akbar in 1571 as his imperial capital, this "City of Victory" was abandoned just 14 years later due to severe water shortages.

  • Mehtab Bagh: Established by Emperor Babur in 1528 as the last of eleven riverfront gardens, this "Moonlight Garden" was later redesigned by Shah Jahan to perfectly align with the Taj Mahal. It served as the ultimate vantage point to view the white marble mausoleum’s reflection in the Yamuna River under the glow of the full moon.
