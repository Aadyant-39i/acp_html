Goa

Goa is a coastal treasure shaped by 450 years of Portuguese rule, featuring a unique fusion of Mediterranean architecture and vibrant Indian traditions. It is renowned for its UNESCO-listed cathedrals, historic spice plantations, and golden beaches that stretch across a sun-drenched tropical landscape.


  • Basilica of Bom Jesus: Completed in 1605, this UNESCO site enshrines the remains of St. Francis Xavier and is a world-renowned landmark of baroque architecture.


  • Fontainhas: Established in the late 1700s, this historic Latin Quarter in Panjim preserves the city's Mediterranean influence with its vibrant, colourful heritage houses.


  • Fort Aguada: Built in 1612 by the Portuguese to guard against Dutch and Maratha invaders, it features a 19th-century lighthouse and a massive freshwater reservoir.


  • Se Cathedral: One of the largest churches in Asia, it was built in 1619 to celebrate a Portuguese victory and is famous for its "Golden Bell" that can be heard for miles. One of its towers was demolished because of a lightning strike way back in 1776!


  • Calangute Beach: Known as the "Queen of Beaches," this massive 7-km stretch became a global hippie icon in the 1960s after centuries as a quiet fishing village. Today, it serves as Goa's busiest hub, famous for its high-energy water sports, bustling local markets, and legendary beach shacks.
