Pizza

Pizza is a popular Italian dish that consists of a round, flat base of dough topped with various ingredients such as tomato sauce, cheese, vegetables, and meats. It is typically baked in an oven and can be served in a variety of styles, including thin crust, deep dish, and stuffed crust. Pizza is enjoyed by people all over the world and is often considered a comfort food.

Pizza originated in Naples, Italy, in the late 18th century as a quick, affordable meal for working-class people, with modern roots linked to Rafaelle Esposito and the 1889 creation of the Margherita. Today, it is a global phenomenon with a ~$128 billion market, with Americans consuming 350 slices per second and over 90% eating it monthly

Pizza ingredients consist of four main components: dough, sauce, cheese, and toppings. Essential dough ingredients are flour, water, yeast, salt, and olive oil. The most common cheese is mozzarella, while traditional red sauce contains tomatoes, herbs, and garlic. Common toppings range from pepperoni, pineapple and vegetables to gourmet items like pesto and arugula.

Pizza Styles

Pizza styles vary widely across the world, with popular types including Neapolitan, New York, Chicago deep-dish, and Sicilian. Neapolitan pizza features a thin, soft crust with simple toppings, while New York-style pizza has a thin, crispy crust and is often sold by the slice. Chicago deep-dish pizza is known for its thick crust and generous toppings, and Sicilian pizza is characterized by its thick, rectangular crust and hearty toppings.

Popular Pizza Chains

Domino's Pizza, Pizza Hut, Papa John's, and Little Caesars are the world's most popular pizza chains, recognized as the "Big Four" for their immense global footprints. Other significant international brands include Sbarro, California Pizza Kitchen, Blaze Pizza, and Dodo Pizza, which are expanding rapidly across Asia, Europe, and Canada
